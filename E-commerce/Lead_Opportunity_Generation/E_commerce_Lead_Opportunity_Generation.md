**Client description:**
The company is a fast-growing mid-sized e-commerce retailer operating in a competitive digital marketplace. It sells consumer products directly to customers through its website and mobile platform. The business relies heavily on digital channels such as paid advertising, social media campaigns, search marketing, and email campaigns to attract customers.
Over the past few years, the company has successfully increased website traffic and brand visibility. However, revenue growth has not scaled proportionally with traffic growth.

The company follows a direct-to-consumer (D2C) online sales model. The customer journey typically includes:
1.	Customer visits website through ads or search
2.	Browses products
3.	Raises enquiry (price, availability, delivery, return policy)
4.	Adds to cart
5.	Completes purchase
Revenue is generated primarily through product sales. Profitability depends on:
•	Conversion rate
•	Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
•	Average order value (AOV)
•	Repeat purchase rate

3️⃣ Enquiry Management Process
Objective: Address customer doubts and reduce purchase friction.
Enquiry Channels: Chat, Email, Call center, WhatsApp, Contact form
Process Flow:
1.	Customer raises enquiry.
2.	Ticket is generated in CRM/helpdesk.
3.	Support agent manually reviews enquiry.
4.	Agent checks:
o	Product availability
o	Pricing
o	Delivery timeline
o	Offers
5.	Response sent to customer.
6.	Ticket closed.
Challenges:
•	Delayed response time
•	No prioritization based on purchase intent
•	Limited personalization
•	No predictive insight on conversion probability

2.	Current Situation
The company generates a high volume of leads every month through marketing campaigns. However:
•	A significant percentage of visitors do not convert.
•	Many customers raise enquiries but do not complete purchases.
•	Cart abandonment rates are high.
•	Enquiry handling is partially manual and delayed.
•	Marketing spend is increasing but ROI is inconsistent.
The management team recognizes that while traffic acquisition is strong, the real business challenge lies in converting leads and enquiries into revenue.
